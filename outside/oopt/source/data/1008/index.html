Geographical location

Northern parts of Volga Uplands; watersheds of the Bezdna River (a tributary of the Sura) and the Karla River (a tributary of the Sviyaga); in the broadleaf forest subzone.

Major landscapes and ecosystems

The terrain is zonal-litomorphic origin in transitional zone, with uneven relief and a dense hydrological network on outwash, erosion-denudation, and denudation-outwash plains; intrazonal southern taiga pine forests, coniferous-broadleaf, and broadleaf forests; black alder forests; riparian spruce forests; second-growth birch and aspen forests; pine plantations.

Species diversity

41 species of mammals; 173 species of birds; 16 species of amphibians and reptiles; 19 species of fish; about 800 species of vascular plants.

Rareplants and animals

Birds: shoe-toed snake eagle, imperial eagle, pallid harrier, oystercatcher, eagle-owl, great grey shrike; Fish: spirlin; Insects: Bombus fragrans, hermit beetle, clouded Apollo, Apollo; Plants: red helleborine, Eastern pasqueflower, sword lily, lady bells, Lilium pilosiusculum, etc.

Main ecosystem features

Acombination of ecosystems typical for various natural zones and landscapes of Chuvash resion and adjacent areas of middle Volga; a mosaic communities from boggy to dry.

Natural attractions

Southern boreal pine forests; pine-broadleaf forests with spruce (at southern edge of its range); elements of boreal, pine, and nemoral flora with hight biodiversity; oak forests with ash (on the eastern edge of its range); steppe pine forests; the Bezdna River and its tributaries and adjacent old-growth forests; sandy terraces and ridges; bogs in depressions between ridges; small floodplain meadows with populations of rare plants and animals; Sursko-Volga Site with a 16-17th century abatis.
